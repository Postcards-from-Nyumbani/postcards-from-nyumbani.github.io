There is a specific quality of light in Nairobi at dusk that I have not found anywhere else.

It arrives without announcement — the sun does not set dramatically here the way travel writing would have you believe. Instead, it simply *withdraws*. And in the four minutes between its leaving and the streetlights deciding to respond, the city turns a colour I can only describe as the inside of a promise.

This photograph was taken on the last roll of Kodak Portra 400 I imported before the duty became unreasonable. I did not meter. I did not bracket. I stood on the balcony, smelled the charcoal fires starting in the valley below, and pressed the shutter once.

---

*The print hangs above my desk now. When I look at it on the difficult days, I remember that the city has survived every single one of its blue hours so far.*
